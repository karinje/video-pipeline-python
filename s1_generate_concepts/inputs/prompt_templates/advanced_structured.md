## AD STYLE GUIDE

### A - HUMOR: The Escalating Absurdity

**CRITICAL: Humor must land. Ultra think about what makes this funny? Then think again whether most humans would find it funny.**

**Narrative Arc**: Normal â†’ Problem â†’ Escalation â†’ Absurdity Peak â†’ Resolution

#### Scene 1: Normal Setup with Quirky Hint (3-5 seconds)
**Purpose**: Establish seemingly normal situation with subtle hint something's off
- Show relatable character or everyday scenario
- Plant seed of upcoming chaos (visual detail, character quirk, odd element)
- Tone should feel light, not serious
- **Example**: Person confidently preparing for simple task with one odd detail

#### Scene 2: Small Manageable Problem (4-6 seconds)
**Purpose**: Introduce the problem that will spiral
- Problem appears minor and solvable at first
- Character reacts with confidence or mild concern
- Audience thinks "this is fixable"
- **Example**: Small mishap occurs, character attempts obvious solution

#### Scene 3: Problem Spirals Ridiculously (5-7 seconds)
**Purpose**: Escalate absurdity, pile on complications
- Original solution backfires or makes things worse
- Can show quick cuts of attempts failing
- Situation becomes increasingly ridiculous
- Physical comedy or visual gags intensify
- **Example**: Domino effect of disasters, each more absurd than the last

#### Scene 4: Absurdity Peaks with Comic Twist (4-6 seconds)
**Purpose**: Deliver the punchline or peak of chaos
- The most ridiculous/unexpected moment
- Should make audience laugh out loud
- Often involves surprising reveal or unexpected consequence
- Peak energy and comedic timing
- **Example**: Character's elaborate fix results in spectacular failure or ironic situation

#### Scene 5: Product Solves Chaos with Wit (3-5 seconds)
**Purpose**: Brand provides elegant/funny solution
- Product arrives and instantly solves the mess
- Integration should feel clever, not forced
- Final callback or visual joke reinforces brand
- Leave audience smiling
- **Example**: Brand product does in one simple action what character couldn't in 4 scenes

### B - SENTIMENT: The Hidden Connection

**Narrative Arc**: Connection â†’ Hidden Struggle â†’ Sacrifice â†’ Reveal â†’ Payoff

#### Scene 1: Character in Touching Moment (4-6 seconds)
**Purpose**: Establish connection with audience and hint at deeper story
- Show character in relatable, tender moment
- Should feel genuine, not manipulative
- Plant visual clues about hidden context
- Soft, warm tone from the start
- **Example**: Parent doing something simple but meaningful; elderly person preparing carefully

#### Scene 2: Selfless Struggle or Act of Love (5-7 seconds)
**Purpose**: Show character's effort or sacrifice without revealing why
- Character works hard, saves, prepares, or endures something
- Audience sees the effort but doesn't understand full context yet
- Build sympathy and curiosity
- Can show passage of time or repeated actions
- **Example**: Someone working extra shifts, carefully saving money, making sacrifices

#### Scene 3: Sacrifice Deepens, Context Still Hidden (5-7 seconds)
**Purpose**: Intensify audience investment before the reveal
- Show more of the struggle or dedication
- Obstacles or challenges appear
- Audience investment peaks - "Why are they doing this?"
- Visual storytelling carries emotion
- **Example**: Character persisting despite setbacks, continued dedication over time

#### Scene 4: Reveal Hidden Meaning (4-6 seconds)
**Purpose**: The reveal that recontextualizes everything
- The "why" is finally revealed
- Should be unexpected but make perfect sense in hindsight
- This is the tears/goosebumps moment

- **Example**: The purpose revealed - surprise for loved one, secret sacrifice, hidden connection

#### Scene 5: Payoff + Brand (3-5 seconds)
**Purpose**: Beautiful resolution showing brand's role
- Character achieves their goal

- Integration feels organic to the emotion

### C - ACHIEVEMENT: The Underdog Triumph

**Narrative Arc**: Challenge â†’ Doubt â†’ Grind â†’ Triumph â†’ Meaning

#### Scene 1: Underdog Faces Challenge (4-6 seconds)
**Purpose**: Introduce character and seemingly impossible goal
- Show relatable underdog (age, ability, circumstance makes them unlikely)
- Establish the challenge or dream
- Immediate connection - audience roots for them
- Visual contrast: underdog vs. challenge scale
- **Example**: Older person at gym entrance; beginner facing experts; person with disadvantage eyeing goal

#### Scene 2: Doubters and Obstacles (5-7 seconds)
**Purpose**: Stack odds against protagonist, create tension
- Show skepticism from others (looks, comments, dismissal)
- Highlight legitimate obstacles and difficulties
- Can show initial failure or struggle
- Audience feels defensive protectiveness for character
- **Example**: Others' skeptical glances; first attempts fail badly; physical/circumstantial barriers

#### Scene 3: The Grind (5-7 seconds)
**Purpose**: Show dedication and persistence over time

- Passage of time indicated visually

#### Scene 4: Triumphant Moment (4-6 seconds)
**Purpose**: Achieve the goal with unexpected twist
- Character succeeds at the challenge
- Twist: unexpected supporters, bigger impact than expected, community element

- Peak moment - goosebumps
- **Example**: Crossing finish line as former doubters cheer; achieving goal inspires others; victory bigger than anticipated

### E - REVERSAL: The Perspective Shift

**Narrative Arc**: Misdirection â†’ Building Evidence â†’ Assumption Solidifies â†’ Flip â†’ New Perspective

#### Scene 1: Scene Appearing One Way (4-6 seconds)
**Purpose**: Establish scenario that audience will misinterpret
- Show situation that appears to be one thing
- Plant visual clues that support false interpretation
- Audience forms immediate assumption
- Tone matches the assumed scenario
- **Example**: Person acting suspiciously; tense situation; concerning behavior; seemingly negative scenario

#### Scene 2: Tension Builds from Misinterpretation (5-7 seconds)
**Purpose**: Deepen audience's false assumption
- Add details that "confirm" initial interpretation
- Build tension or emotion based on misunderstanding
- Audience becomes more convinced of wrong narrative
- Can show reactions from others in scene
- **Example**: More "evidence" of negative situation; others react to perceived scenario; tension escalates

#### Scene 3: Assumption Solidifies (5-7 seconds)
**Purpose**: Peak misdirection before the flip
- Maximum investment in false interpretation
- Audience is certain they understand what's happening
- Last pieces of "evidence" fall into place
- Peak intensity based on wrong assumption
- **Example**: Situation reaches critical point; character behavior confirms false narrative; audience fully invested

#### Scene 4: The Reality-Flipping Reveal (4-6 seconds)
**Purpose**: Reveal true context, reframe everything
- Sudden reveal shows what was really happening
- All previous scenes gain entirely new meaning
- "Oh!" or "Wait, WHAT?" moment
- Should feel surprising but make perfect sense
- **Example**: Camera pulls back; key detail revealed; true context shown; perspective completely shifts

#### Scene 5: New Perspective + Brand Message (3-5 seconds)
**Purpose**: Audience processes new understanding with brand
- Show true resolution of actual situation
- Brand message aligns with the real story
- Audience reflects on assumptions challenged
- Meaningful or clever tagline reinforces lesson
- **Example**: Brand connected to real positive story; message about not judging/seeing differently; powerful reframe
